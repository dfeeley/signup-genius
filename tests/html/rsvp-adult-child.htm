Sierra Buttes Campout

Pack your bags and get ready to bag a peak!

The iconic Sierra Buttes is our October destination.  Nestled on the southern end of the stunning Lakes Basin (NW of Tahoe) this 8,857-foot Sierra peak juts up almost 4,700 feet above the scenic Yuba-Donner Scenic Byway and boasts an unimpeded 360-degree view from its summit.  It is a popular side trip for thru-hikers tackling the Pacific Trail. 

This will be an on-trail hike for all skill levels with no technical requirements and no commitment other than walking up some alternating mild-to-not-as-mild slopes at elevation. We can make this work for anyone willing to give it a go!

From our forward base of operations - Packsaddle Camp near beautiful and trout-filled Packer Lake. It is roughly a 2.5-mile trek to the fire lookout at the top of the peak (approx. 5 miles round trip). 

Dates:  Sat Oct 15 - Mon Oct 17

Location: Packsaddle Campground. Potable water and vault toilets. 

Cost:  TBD (will be published when the final headcount is established)

RSVP:  Please RSVP by October 3rd.  Any responses thereafter may not be accepted for planning purposes.

Saturday we will set up camp and that afternoon will be free to fish (Packer Lake is next door and is stocked with rainbows and brown trout), explore the nearby Sardine Lakes (stunning views of Sierra Buttes), work on patrol-led activities, etc.  There is also a short hike to two twin pristine alpine lakes called Tamarack Lakes. 

Sunday will be the Sierra Buttes summit hike followed by other activities as determined by patrols and uniformed leaders.  Please check out the below links to get psyched (and maybe alleviate any trepidation if this is a totally new experience for you - little chihuahuas routinely make it to the summit!). 

Monday will be camp cleanup and departure. This is the very last day the campground is open for the season, so there will be no rush to leave for those who may want to linger. 

In addition to your normal car camping gear, please bring comfortable shoes to hike in (we will only need small day packs for the ascent, so no need to bring your super stiff backpacking boots, especially if they aren’t broken in) and plenty of layers to manage body temp on the hike. Also be prepared for cold temps at night. This is mid-October in the Sierra and Packsaddle is location at 6,000 feet. Snow is not out of the question (wishful thinking), but more likely is a sunny blue sky summit day with mild daytime temps.

Hope to see you all there!
